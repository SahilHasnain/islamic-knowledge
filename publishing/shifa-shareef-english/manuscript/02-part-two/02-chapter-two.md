# Chapter Two: Love For Huzoor Is Necessary And Wajib Upon The Ummah

Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> says:

"Say: if your fathers, your sons, your brothers, your wives, your family, and the wealth you have earned..." (`at-Tawbah: 24`)

This verse establishes the necessity of loving Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, its obligatory and important nature, and that Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> is the true one most deserving of love. Here Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> gave a severe warning: if you regard love for your family, children, and wealth as greater than love for Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> and His Messenger <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, then:

"Wait until Allah brings His command." (`at-Tawbah: 24`)

At the end of the verse, such people were called sinful, and it was made clear that they are among those misguided people whom Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> did not grant the ability to receive guidance.

It is narrated from Hazrat Anas <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> that Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "None of you will be a believer until I become more beloved to him than his children, his father, and all people."

On another occasion, he said that a person is not complete in iman until he regards him as more beloved than his own self. At that time, Hazrat Umar Faruq <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> submitted: "By the One Who revealed the Book upon you, now you are more beloved to me than my own self." Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "O Umar, now you have become a complete believer."

Hazrat Sahl <sup>رحمة الله تعالى عليه</sup> said that the one who does not see the authority and rule of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> over himself in every state, and who regards his own self as his own property, will not taste the sweetness of the Sunnah of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>. This is because Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "None of you can be a believer until I am more beloved to him than his own self."

## First Section: The Reward For Loving Huzoor

It is narrated from Hazrat Anas <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> that a man came to the service of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> and asked: "When will the Day of Judgment come?" Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "What have you prepared for it?" He replied: "I do not have an abundance of prayers, fasting, or charity for it, but I love Allah and His Messenger <sup>عليه السلام</sup> most of all." Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "You will be with the one whom you love."

It is narrated from Hazrat Safwan ibn Qudamah that he migrated toward the Prophet <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, then came into his blessed court and submitted: "Ya RasoolAllah, give me your blessed hand so that I may pledge allegiance." Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> extended his blessed hand. He submitted: "Ya RasoolAllah, I love you." Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "A person is with the one whom he loves."

It is narrated from Hazrat Ali <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> that Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "Whoever loves Hasan and Husayn <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنهما</sup>, and their father and mother, will be with me on the Day of Judgment in my rank."

It is narrated that a man came to the court of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> and submitted: "Ya RasoolAllah, you are more beloved to me than my family and wealth. I remember you in my heart, and until I see Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> with my own eyes, I do not find patience and peace. When I remember my death and separation from you, I know that you will enter Paradise and be in high stations with the Prophets. If I enter Paradise, I fear that I will not be able to see you."

Then Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> revealed this verse:

"And whoever obeys Allah and the Messenger, they will be with those upon whom Allah bestowed favor: the Prophets, the Siddiqin, the martyrs, and the righteous. And what excellent companions they are." (`an-Nisa: 69`)

## Second Section: Statements Of The Righteous Predecessors About Loving Huzoor

It is narrated from Hazrat Abu Hurairah <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> that Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "After me, people will come who will love me and will wish that they could see me even in exchange for their wealth and children."

It is transmitted from Hazrat Amr ibn al-As <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> that no one was more beloved to him than Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>.

It is narrated from Ubaydah bint Khalid that when her father Khalid would go to his bed, he would mention his longing for Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> and his love for the Companions, naming them one by one. He would say: "These people are my root and lineage," meaning that his heart inclined toward them. He would say: "My longing is long. O my Lord, take my soul toward them." He would continue saying this until sleep overcame him.

Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddiq <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> submitted to Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>: "By the One Who sent you with the truth, Abu Talib accepting Islam would be more beloved to me than my father Abu Quhafah accepting Islam, because Abu Talib accepting Islam would be a means of coolness for your blessed eyes."

Hazrat Umar <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> said something similar to Hazrat Abbas <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup>: that Abu Talib accepting Islam would have been more beloved to him than his own father accepting Islam, because that was beloved to Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>.

In one battle, the father, brother, and husband of an Ansari woman were all martyred. When she was informed, she asked about Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>: "How is Huzoor?" When she saw Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, she said: "Now every calamity is easy for me."

Hazrat Ali <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> was asked: "How was your love for Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>?" He replied: "By Allah, Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> was more beloved to me than my wealth, my children, my parents, and cold water at the time of thirst."

In one narration, the foot of Hazrat Abdullah ibn Umar <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنهما</sup> became numb. Someone said to him: "Remember the one most beloved to you; it will become well." He loudly said, "Ya Muhammad <sup>عليه السلام</sup>," and immediately his foot became well.

Similarly, when the time of Hazrat Bilal's passing arrived, his wife said: "What grief!" He said: "What joy! Tomorrow I will meet my beloved Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> and his group."

It is narrated that a woman said to Hazrat Aishah <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنها</sup>: "Open the grave of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> for me." It was opened for her, and she began to weep until she passed away there.

After the martyrdom of Hazrat Ibn Zubayr <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنهما</sup>, Hazrat Ibn Umar <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنهما</sup> stood by him, sought forgiveness for him, and said: "By Allah, I know well that you were one who fasted much, stood much in prayer at night, and loved Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> and His Messenger <sup>عليه السلام</sup>."

## Third Section: The Signs Of Loving Huzoor

Not everyone who claims love for Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> is truthful unless the signs of love are found in him.

The first sign is that he follows Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> and acts upon his Sunnah. He follows the sayings and actions of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, fulfills his commands, avoids what he forbade, and takes counsel from the etiquette of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> in every state. Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> says:

"O Beloved, say: O people, if you love Allah, then become obedient to me; Allah will love you." (`Aal Imran: 31`)

Another sign of love is that one gives preference to what Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> explained over one's own desire. Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> says:

"And those who had settled in the city and in iman before them..." (`al-Hashr: 9`)

Hazrat Anas ibn Malik <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> narrated that Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said to him: "O my son, if you are able to spend your morning and evening in such a state that your heart is pure and clean from rancor toward everyone, then do so." Then he said: "O my son, this is my Sunnah. Whoever keeps my Sunnah alive has loved me, and whoever loves me will be with me in Paradise."

Therefore, whoever possesses this quality will be complete in love for Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> and His Messenger. Whoever opposes some of these matters, his love will be deficient to that extent. His love will be deficient, but he will not leave the circle of love.

The proof of this is that regarding the man who was punished for drinking wine, and people cursed him, Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "Do not curse him, for he loves Allah and His Messenger." (`Bukhari`)

Another sign of love is that one mentions the Beloved often, because a person mentions frequently the one whom he loves.

Another sign is longing to see Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, because every lover longs to see his beloved.

Another sign is showing great reverence when Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> is mentioned, and when his blessed name is mentioned, expressing utmost humility and lowliness.

It is narrated that when the Companions mentioned Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, they did so with immense reverence, their hair would stand on end, and they would weep.

Another sign of love is that if one loves anyone, he loves him for the sake of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>; that he loves the Companions and the Ahl al-Bayt; and that he holds enmity toward those who harbor hatred and hostility toward them.

Regarding Hazrat Hasan and Hazrat Husayn <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنهما</sup>, Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "O Allah, I love them, so You love them as well."

He also said: "Beware, beware regarding my Companions. Do not make them targets for your personal aims after me. Whoever loves them does so because of love for me, and whoever shows enmity to them does so because of enmity toward me. Whoever harms them has harmed me, and whoever harms me has certainly harmed Allah. And whoever harms Allah, soon Allah will seize him."

Regarding Hazrat Fatimah <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنها</sup>, he said: "She is a part of me. Whatever angers her angers me."

Because of love for Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, love for the Ansar, love for the Companions, and even love for the people of Arabia has been mentioned.

Hazrat Anas <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> said: "I saw Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> searching for pieces of gourd in the dish. From that day onward, I always loved gourd."

Among the signs of love is that one keeps enmity with those whom Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> and His Messenger <sup>عليه السلام</sup> kept enmity with, and that one distances himself from the person who opposes his Sunnah and introduces new matters into the religion.

This was the state of the Companions: they killed their own friends, fathers, and children, and fought against them. Abdullah, the son of Abdullah ibn Ubayy, who was the leader of the hypocrites, submitted to Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>: "If you wish, I will cut off my father's head and present it to you."

Among the signs of love is that one loves the Qur'an brought by him. Loving it means reciting it, acting upon it, understanding it well, loving its way, and not going beyond its limits.

Another sign of love is that one deals with the Ummah of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> with compassion and kindness, teaches them good, strives for their well-being, and removes their harms, just as Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> was `Ra'uf` and `Rahim` toward the Muslims.

Among the signs of complete love for him is that the one who claims it should be detached from the world, accustomed to concern, and give preference to the poor.

Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said to Hazrat Abu Sa'id al-Khudri <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup>: "Whoever among you loves me, poverty will rush toward him more swiftly than a flood descending from a high place in the wilderness or from a mountain."

## Fourth Section: The Meaning And Reality Of Love

Scholars differed regarding the meaning of loving Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, and the explanation and intent of loving Allah and His Prophet. In reality, there is no real disagreement, though they differ in describing its state.

Hazrat Sufyan <sup>رحمة الله تعالى عليه</sup> said that love is the name of following the Messenger.

Some scholars said that love means considering the support and aid of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> necessary, removing those who oppose the Sunnah, following the Sunnah, and fearing opposition to the Sunnah.

Some said that love is to keep mentioning the beloved constantly. Some said that sacrificing oneself for the beloved is love. Some said that it is longing for the beloved. Some said that it is to do whatever the beloved says. Some said that love is the inclination of the heart toward agreement.

All the causes of love are gathered in love for Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>. Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> is highest in beauty, perfection, and character. His favors and benevolence upon his Ummah have already been described. He is `Ra'uf` and `Rahim` toward the Muslims and mercy for all the worlds.

What favor could be greater than his favor? When one reflects, it becomes clear that in reality Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> is the one most deserving of Shari'ah love. A servant loves the one who does him a favor once or twice in this world, or saves him from a calamity, and that too from a calamity that will eventually end. In contrast, Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> granted us blessings that will never end and saved us from such punishment of Hell that will never perish. Therefore, he is most deserving of love.

## Fifth Section: Sincere Goodwill Toward Huzoor Is Wajib

Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> says:

"Nor is there blame upon those who do not possess the means to spend, when they remain sincere to Allah and His Messenger. There is no way of blame against the people of goodness, and Allah is Forgiving, Merciful." (`at-Tawbah: 91`)

The commentators said that sincere goodwill toward Allah and His Messenger means being Muslim outwardly and inwardly with complete character.

It is narrated from Hazrat Tamim ad-Dari <sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup> that Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup> said: "Indeed, religion is sincere goodwill. Without doubt, religion is sincere goodwill. Certainly, religion is sincere goodwill." The Companions asked: "For whom?" He said: "For Allah, His Book, His Messenger, and the leaders of the Muslims."

Sincere goodwill toward the Noble Qur'an is to believe in it, act upon whatever is in it, and recite it properly. Sincere goodwill toward the Messenger is to affirm him and accept whatever he commands or forbids.

Abu Bakr al-Ajurri <sup>رحمة الله تعالى عليه</sup> says that during his outward lifetime, the Companions showed sincere goodwill by always coming forward for his support and aid. After his passing, sincere goodwill is to honor and revere Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, to hold firmly to his love, to continually strive to learn his Sunnah, to gain understanding in his Shari'ah, to love his family and Companions, and to keep enmity with those who oppose his Sunnah.

Imam Qushayri <sup>رحمة الله تعالى عليه</sup> relates that Amr ibn Layth, a ruler of Khurasan, once climbed a mountain and looked upon his large army. He became pleased and wished: "If only I had been in the service of Huzoor <sup>عليه السلام</sup>, I would have aided and supported him." Because of this wish, Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup> forgave him.

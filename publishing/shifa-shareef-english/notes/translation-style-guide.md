# Translation Style Guide

## Tone

- Use respectful Islamic English.
- Preserve adab when referring to Allah <sup>عز وجل</sup>, Huzoor ﷺ, the Prophets <sup>عليهم السلام</sup>, the Sahabah, and the righteous.
- Avoid blunt or casual wording where reverential wording is more appropriate.

## Honorifics

- Do not translate honorifics like `rahimahullahu ta'ala`.
- Use `<sup>رحمة الله تعالى عليه</sup>` for righteous male scholars and saints.
- Use `<sup>رضي الله تعالى عنه</sup>` for a male Companion.
- Use `<sup>رضي الله تعالى عنها</sup>` for a female Companion.
- Use `<sup>رضي الله تعالى عنهما</sup>` for two Companions or paired references.
- Use `<sup>عليه السلام</sup>` for Prophets and angels.
- Use `<sup>عز وجل</sup>` after Allah where appropriate.
- Use `ﷺ` for salawat upon Huzoor ﷺ where it reads naturally.

## Preferred Terms

- Use `unschooled Prophet` for the Prophet ﷺ where the source discusses not being taught by worldly teachers.
- Use `unlettered people` where the source means ordinary `anpadh` people.
- Use `hearts find peace`, not `hearts find rest`.
- Preserve terms such as `durood`, `salam`, `taqwa`, `zuhd`, `sabr`, `wudu`, `sajdah`, and `Ummah` unless a short explanation is needed.

## Source Edition Wording

- Replace Roman Urdu edition wording with English edition wording where speaking to the reader.
- Keep source-chain context clear where relevant: this English edition is prepared from the Roman Urdu edition, which itself was based on Urdu.

## Review Checks

Before considering a batch reviewed, scan for these stale or forbidden phrases:

- `may Allah have mercy`
- `unlettered Prophet`
- `unschooled people`
- `hearts find rest`
- `Roman Urdu was`
- `converting this book into Roman Urdu`
